My communication is a mess, I admit it.

Not that I have particularly special things to say, but I'd still like to do it in a dignified way.

This blog has often hosted extremely intimate things, other times extremely technical things, and other times still, extremely useless things. For a person, it doesn't make sense to follow this kind of blog, because it doesn't have a real theme — and as long as it's about me, it doesn't even need one.

With the launch of the [HappyBackending](https://happybackending.com) project, social channels have multiplied, and my online presence needs to be more coherent and professional. This doesn't mean I have to shut down this blog — just separate the waters. I'd also like to give third parties the ability to write technical articles in the future, and I can't do that with a personal blog.

Technical content will be migrated to [HappyBackending](https://happybackending.com), while personal content will remain here.

I'm trying to think more broadly about social media usage. Nowadays social media have literally multiplied; it's not possible, and it doesn't make sense, to be present on all of them. Compared to a few years ago, there's fierce competition: TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, YouTube, Twitch, Discord, Telegram, WhatsApp, Snapchat, Pinterest, Reddit, Medium, Substack, Patreon, OnlyFans, Threads, Mastodon — and the list goes on.

Presence needs to be more widespread but more focused, tailored to the context.

Cheers and kisses.
